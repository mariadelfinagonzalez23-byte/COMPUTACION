# Ad Copy Generator

Ads die in the scroll. The job is to earn the next second of attention, then the next, then convert. This skill writes ads that do that without resorting to spammy tricks.

## Step 1: Gather the inputs

Before writing, get:

1. **Product / offer** — what's being sold, at what price, with what terms?
2. **Target audience** — demographic, psychographic, pain points
3. **Platform** — FB/IG, Google Search, YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn, etc.
4. **Unique angle or mechanism** — what makes this different?
5. **Objections** — what stops people from buying?
6. **Landing page** — where does the ad send traffic? (ideally read it)
7. **Existing winners or losers** — if they've run ads before, what worked?

## Step 2: Pick angles before writing copy

Don't write 10 variations of the same angle. Write 1 variation each of 5 different angles. The angle is the strategic bet — the copy is the execution.

Common angle types:

- **Problem/agitation** — name the pain sharply
- **Dream outcome** — paint the after-state
- **Social proof** — results, testimonials, "X people can't be wrong"
- **Contrarian** — "Everyone says X. That's wrong because Y."
- **Mechanism** — "The [specific method] that makes [result] possible"
- **Urgency / scarcity** — only if genuinely true
- **Before/after** — transformation-focused
- **Us vs. them** — positioning against competitors or incumbent solutions

## Platform-specific structure

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
- **Primary text** — 125 chars shows before "See more." First line is the whole ballgame.
- **Headline** — 27 chars ideal; reinforces the primary text
- **Description** — 27 chars; often gets cut, don't rely on it
- **Structure:** Hook → substance → CTA
- Test: video ads, static ads, UGC-style, carousel

### Google Search
- **Headlines** — 30 chars each, 3-15 provided for responsive search ads
- **Descriptions** — 90 chars each, 2-4 provided
- **Match the keyword intent** — if they searched "[product] reviews," don't send them to a signup page
- **Include:** primary keyword, a differentiator, a CTA, social proof

### TikTok / Reels
- **Copy:** minimal — 100 chars or less, the video does the work
- **Hook:** in the video (first 1-3 seconds) — visual + text overlay + voice
- **CTAs:** "Link in bio," "Swipe up," "Learn more"

### YouTube (skippable)
- **First 5 seconds** — earn the non-skip
- **Hook at second 0** — address the viewer's problem or promise
- **Mid:** mechanism / proof / offer
- **End:** CTA with urgency

### LinkedIn
- **Tone:** more professional, benefit-led
- **Primary text:** 150 words max that load naturally
- **Target by job title / industry / company size** — copy should speak to that role's pain

## Hook patterns that work

- **Specific number:** "I spent $47K on Facebook ads so you don't have to"
- **Contradiction:** "Stop drinking green smoothies. Here's why."
- **Problem in the wild:** [specific scene] — "Your shoulders are in your ears reading this, aren't they?"
- **Question to the prospect:** "Ever finish a meeting and forget what got decided?"
- **Bold claim + proof:** "$10K MRR in 90 days — here's the ad that did it"
- **Callout:** "To the founder still doing their own bookkeeping:"

## Voice and style

- Short sentences. Line breaks matter.
- Concrete > abstract. "30-minute bath" beats "relaxing bath experience."
- Second person ("you") — always
- Don't bury the benefit. Lead with it.
- Match the platform's native tone (IG is not LinkedIn)

## What to avoid

- "Revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "next-gen" — dead words
- Emoji walls (one or two, max, and only where native)
- "Game-changer," "unleash," "harness" — AI tells
- Overpromising (gets accounts banned)
- Fake urgency ("only 3 left!" when there aren't)
- Starting with the brand name — nobody cares yet

## Compliance quick-check

For Meta especially, avoid:
- Personal attributes ("Are YOU struggling with diabetes?") — use "people who..." or "if you're..."
- Before/after images for weight loss, cosmetics, health
- Overly specific income claims without disclaimers
- Discriminatory targeting phrasing

## Output format

Deliver a set of 5 angles, each with:

```
ANGLE [N]: [Angle name, e.g., "Contrarian mechanism"]
Strategic bet: [Why this angle, in one sentence]

Primary text / Headline / Description:
[Copy formatted for the platform]

Creative direction (if applicable):
[Suggested visual or video concept — static image, UGC talking-head, stop-motion, etc.]

Expected best-for: [cold / retargeting / LAL / etc.]
```

End with a **testing plan** — which angles to test first, what to look at in the data, and how to scale winners.

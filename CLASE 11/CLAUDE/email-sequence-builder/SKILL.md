# Email Sequence Builder

Write email sequences that feel like a thoughtful human sending one email at a time — not a marketing machine blasting a list.

## Step 1: Identify the sequence type

Ask (or infer) which of these the user needs:

- **Welcome series** — new subscriber onboarding, typically 3-5 emails
- **Nurture sequence** — long-term relationship building, 5-10+ emails over weeks
- **Launch sequence** — pre-launch, launch, close — typically 5-8 emails over 7-14 days
- **Re-engagement** — win back cold subscribers, 3-4 emails
- **Cart abandonment / browse abandonment** — 2-4 emails over 1-7 days
- **Post-purchase** — upsell, education, review request
- **Cold outreach sequence** — B2B prospecting, 4-6 touches

Different types use different frameworks. Don't apply a launch formula to a welcome series.

## Step 2: Gather context

Before drafting:

1. **Product / offer** — what's being sold or promoted?
2. **Audience** — who's on this list? How did they join?
3. **Tone** — casual, professional, personality-driven?
4. **Sender** — is this from a person or a brand?
5. **Goal of the sequence** — sale, activation, education, reply?
6. **Any existing brand voice** — paste in examples if possible

## Step 3: Apply the right framework

### Welcome series (3-5 emails)
1. **Email 1 (immediate):** Deliver what they signed up for. Set expectations. Ask a low-friction question.
2. **Email 2 (day 1-2):** Origin story or founding "why." Make it personal.
3. **Email 3 (day 3-5):** Best content — most-loved blog post, video, or resource.
4. **Email 4 (day 6-8):** Soft offer — introduce the product/service as a solution to the problem they care about.
5. **Email 5 (day 10-14):** Social proof + stronger CTA.

### Launch sequence (5-8 emails over 7-14 days)
- **Pre-launch:** Tease the problem, share a story, hint at what's coming
- **Launch day:** Open cart, clear value, clear CTA
- **Mid-launch:** Objections, FAQs, testimonials
- **Close:** Urgency (last day, last hours) — but only if it's true

### Nurture
- Mix of: education, personal stories, curated resources, occasional soft pitches
- Rule of thumb: 4 value emails per 1 sales email

### Re-engagement
- **Email 1:** "Did we lose you?" — direct, curious, no pitch
- **Email 2:** Deliver real value (free resource, not a pitch)
- **Email 3:** "Should I remove you?" — polite opt-out

## Email anatomy

Every email needs:

- **Subject line** — under 50 chars, curiosity or benefit, never clickbait
- **Preview text** — complements the subject, doesn't repeat it
- **Opener** — personal, specific, avoids "I hope this email finds you well"
- **One main idea** — not a newsletter round-up
- **One clear CTA** — if you have two, split into two emails
- **P.S.** (optional, often the most-read line) — restate the CTA or add a personal touch

## Voice rules

- Write like you're emailing one friend. Use "I" and "you."
- Short paragraphs. 1-3 sentences each.
- Cut corporate filler. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.
- Subject lines in sentence case, not Title Case
- No emojis unless the brand voice uses them

## Deliverability sanity checks

- Avoid spam triggers: "FREE!!!", excessive punctuation, ALL CAPS subject lines
- Don't include more than 2-3 links in early-sequence emails
- Plain-text feel > heavy HTML (unless it's a transactional/design-heavy brand)

## Output format

For each email in the sequence, provide:

```
EMAIL [N]: [Purpose, e.g., "Day 3 — Best content"]
Send: [Timing, e.g., "3 days after email 2"]

Subject: [subject line]
Preview: [preview text]

---

[Body]

---

Why this works: [1-2 sentences on the strategic role of this email]
```

End with a summary table showing the full sequence at a glance.

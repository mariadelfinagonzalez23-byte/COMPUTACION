# Cold Outreach Writer

Cold outreach fails when it sounds like cold outreach. The goal is a message that feels specific, respectful of the recipient's time, and asks for something reasonable.

## Step 1: Get the essentials

If the user hasn't provided:

1. **Who is the recipient?** (role, company, any public info)
2. **What's the ask?** (meeting, reply, intro, opportunity)
3. **Why this person specifically?** — if they can't answer this, the whole message will feel templated. Push them to find a real reason.
4. **Sender credibility** — who's sending this, what's their angle?
5. **Channel** — email, LinkedIn DM, Twitter/X DM, other?

## The cold outreach formula

Every cold message needs four things:

1. **Relevance signal** (first line) — proof you're writing to them specifically, not blasting a list
2. **Compression** (second section) — why you're reaching out, in 1-2 sentences
3. **The ask** — small, specific, easy to say yes to
4. **Exit ramp** — make it easy to decline politely (counterintuitively, this increases replies)

## Length by channel

- **Cold email:** 50-125 words. If it's over 150, cut it.
- **LinkedIn DM (connect):** Under 300 chars (the free tier limit)
- **LinkedIn DM (after connection):** 50-100 words
- **X/Twitter DM:** 2-3 sentences max

## Opening lines — what works

Good:
- "Saw your post on [specific topic] — the bit about [specific point] lined up with something we're seeing at [context]."
- "Been following your work on [X]. The [specific thing] you built is exactly what we're trying to solve."
- "Your [podcast episode/article/talk] on [X] was one of the few pieces I've seen that got [specific point] right."

Bad:
- "Hope you're well!"
- "I came across your profile and was impressed..."
- "My name is [X] and I'm the CEO of [Y]"
- "I know you're busy so I'll keep this brief" (then writes 400 words)

## The ask — calibrate it to the relationship

Zero relationship → small ask. The ask should be proportional to what they know about you.

Good asks:
- "Worth a 15-min call next week?" (concrete, small)
- "Can I send over a 1-page summary?" (lower commitment than a call)
- "Is [specific person at their company] the right person for this, or should I try someone else?" (respects their time, makes them look good by routing)

Bad asks:
- "Would love to jump on a call to explore synergies" (vague)
- "I'd love to pick your brain" (don't)
- "Looking for 30 minutes to walk you through our deck" (too much)

## Exit ramp

The last line should acknowledge they might not be the right person or the right time:

- "If not the right fit, no worries — a quick 'not now' is super helpful."
- "Happy to take a no, just didn't want to assume."
- "If this isn't relevant, anyone you'd point me to?"

This sounds counterintuitive but it increases reply rates because it removes the social pressure of declining.

## Subject lines (email only)

- Lowercase usually beats Title Case (feels more personal, less marketing)
- 3-5 words
- Avoid: "Quick question," "Opportunity for [Company]," anything that smells like sales

Good:
- "quick question re: [specific thing]"
- "[mutual connection] suggested i reach out"
- "[their company] + [your company]?"
- "following up on your [post/talk]"

## What to avoid

- Over-flattery ("loved your incredible post")
- Automation tells ("I saw that you're the [Title] at [Company]" — obviously merged fields)
- Sending templates that 100 other people are sending
- "Just bumping this" as a follow-up (use a new angle instead)
- Mentioning AI tools or automation in the message itself

## Follow-ups

Default: 2-3 follow-ups, spaced 3-5 days apart. Each one adds something new:

- **Follow-up 1:** Add a piece of value (relevant article, data point, example)
- **Follow-up 2:** The "breakup" — "Assuming this isn't a priority right now. Should I close the loop?"

Don't: "Just floating this back to the top of your inbox."

## Output format

Deliver:

1. **The message** (or messages, for a sequence)
2. **Subject line** (for email) — 2 alternatives
3. **Why this works** — 2-3 bullets on the strategic choices made
4. **What to personalize** — flag any variables the sender needs to fill in

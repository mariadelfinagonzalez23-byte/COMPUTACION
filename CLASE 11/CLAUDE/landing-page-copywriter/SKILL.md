# Landing Page Copywriter

A landing page has one job: move a specific visitor toward a specific action. Every section should pass the "does this help the click?" test.

## Step 1: Define the page

Ask or establish:

1. **Single goal** — what should the visitor do? (sign up, buy, book a call, download) — one goal, not three.
2. **Target visitor** — not "everyone." Who specifically lands here?
3. **Traffic source** — are they coming from an ad, a search result, an email? Their expectations differ.
4. **Offer** — what are they getting, for what, at what cost?
5. **Key objections** — what stops this visitor from acting?
6. **Differentiators** — why this, not the alternatives?
7. **Social proof available** — testimonials, logos, numbers, press?

## The standard structure

Use this skeleton. Adjust based on whether it's a homepage, sales page, or lead capture page.

### 1. Hero (above the fold)
- **Headline** — clear over clever. States the outcome or the promise.
- **Subheadline** — specifies for whom, how, or why
- **Primary CTA** — the action, with a specific verb
- **Hero visual** — screenshot, product shot, or demo video
- **Optional:** one-line social proof ("Trusted by 10,000+ teams")

Headline test: strip everything else. Can the visitor still tell what this is? If not, rewrite.

### 2. Problem / stakes
- Name the pain the visitor is feeling
- Agitate briefly — don't overdo this
- Make them feel understood before you pitch

### 3. Solution introduction
- How the product solves it
- The mechanism — what makes it work
- One clear visual (product shot, diagram, demo)

### 4. Benefits (not features)
- 3-5 benefits, each with a short subhead + 1-2 sentences
- Feature ≠ benefit. "AI-powered" is a feature. "Write posts in 10 minutes instead of 2 hours" is a benefit.
- Pair each benefit with a concrete outcome

### 5. Social proof
- Customer logos
- Testimonials with names, photos, titles, real results
- Numbers: users, revenue generated, time saved, ratings
- Case studies (linked or summarized)

### 6. How it works
- 3-step version: "Sign up → Do X → Get Y"
- Screenshots or diagrams for each step

### 7. Pricing (if applicable)
- Plans side by side
- Highlight the recommended plan
- Annual/monthly toggle if available
- Money-back guarantee if offered

### 8. FAQ
- 5-10 real objections answered plainly
- Handle objections like: "Is it too expensive?", "What if it doesn't work for me?", "How is this different from [competitor]?"

### 9. Final CTA
- Restate the core promise
- Remove friction ("No credit card," "Cancel anytime," "Setup in 5 minutes")
- Same verb as the hero CTA — be consistent

## Headline formulas that work

- **Outcome + timeframe:** "Grow your email list by 10,000 in 90 days"
- **Get [desired outcome] without [pain]:** "Get six-pack abs without giving up pizza"
- **The [adjective] way to [outcome]:** "The lazy way to pay off your mortgage"
- **For [specific audience] who [specific situation]:** "For solo founders tired of cobbling together 7 different tools"
- **[Bold claim] — [proof element]:** "Your writing, 10x faster — and it still sounds like you"

## Writing rules

- Second person ("you"), never "our customers" or "users"
- Concrete > abstract. Numbers, names, specifics.
- Cut every sentence you don't need. Landing pages are dense.
- One idea per section. Don't cram.
- Read it aloud. If you stumble, the visitor will.
- Subheads should be readable on their own — scanners should get the gist from subheads alone

## CTA button copy

Good:
- "Start my free trial"
- "Get the template"
- "Book my call"
- "Try it free for 14 days"

Bad:
- "Submit"
- "Click here"
- "Learn more" (only for low-intent pages)
- "Sign up" (why would I?)

## Social proof placement

- Logos: just below hero (builds immediate trust)
- Testimonials: after benefits (to back up claims)
- Case studies / results: after how-it-works
- Reviews / ratings: near pricing + near final CTA

## What to avoid

- Stock-photo vibes in copy: "empower," "revolutionize," "next-generation"
- Vague promises: "take your business to the next level"
- Too many CTAs pointing to different actions
- "About us" sections above the fold (nobody cares yet)
- Walls of text in feature sections
- Checkmark lists with 15 items (looks like noise)

## Output format

Deliver the copy section by section, matching the skeleton above. For each section:

```
SECTION: [Name]

[Headline / subhead / body as applicable]

Design note: [Visual, layout, or UX suggestion]
```

End with:

1. **3 alternative hero headlines** for A/B testing
2. **Meta title + description** for SEO
3. **Conversion notes** — the 2-3 biggest bets this page is making, and what to test first
